The “score” is the unscaled version of the distance. The “minSCore” is the score of the reference sequence given a specific DeltaMatrix, while “maxScore” is the score of the predicted most diverging sequence given the reference score. The range score is the difference between maxScore and minScore. Taking the case of sequence 2, its asymdist value is the scaled value of its score.

A$asymdist[2] == (A$Score[2]-A$minScore)/A$RangeScore
#> [1] TRUE

The chosen reference sequence is indicated in blue font. Changes in the 5’ end have more effects than in the 3’ end of the sequence, and indeed S1 is more distant to S0 than S2 or S3 are to S0. It seems that S2 is very similar to S3, yet different.

DIST_df <- as.matrix(DIST)

The plot illustrates that phylogenetic distances, due to their calculations that did not account for positional sequence differences, do not differentiate well closely related variants. These appear at the same distance to the reference sequence.
